### 题干

A number is magical if it ends with the digits $11$. Suppose $x \in \{1, \ldots, 10^{100}\}$. What is the probability that $x^3$ is magical? 

### Hint

Any integer can be written in the form $a + 10b$, for $a \in \{0, \ldots, 9\}$ and any integer $b$. Let $x = a + 10b$. Then, 
\[\begin{aligned}
x^3 &= a^3 + 30a^2b + 300 ab^2 + 1000b^3.
\end{aligned}\]

### 解答

Any integer can be written in the form $a + 10b$, for $a \in \{0, \ldots, 9\}$ and any integer $b$. Let $x = a + 10b$. Then, 
\[\begin{aligned}
x^3 &= a^3 + 30a^2b + 300 ab^2 + 1000b^3.
\end{aligned}\]
Notice that the right hand side can only end in $1$ if $a = 1$. Then, we can solve for $b$. 
\[\begin{aligned}
30b + 1 \equiv 11 \mod{100} \\
30b \equiv 10 \mod{100} \\
3b \equiv 1 \mod{100}
\end{aligned}\]
The value of $b$ must end with the digit $7$. We conclude that any value of $x$ whose final two digits are $71$ will be magical. This occurs with probability $\frac{10^{98}}{10^{100}} = 0.01$.
