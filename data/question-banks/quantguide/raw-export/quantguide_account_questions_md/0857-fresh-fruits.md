### 题干

Some fresh fruit was weighted for some domestic purpose. It was found that the apples, pears, and plums exactly balanced each other as follows: One pear and three apples weight the same as $10$ plums; and one apple and six plums weigh the same as one pear.

$$\\$$

How many plums alone would weigh the same as one pear? 

### Hint

Turn the conditions into equations and substitute. 

### 解答

Let $a$, $p$, and $m$ denote the weight of one apple, one pear, and one plum respectively. 

We have:
$$\begin{align*}
&p + 3a = 10m \\ 
&a + 6m = p
\end{align*}$$

The first equation represents one pear and three apples weighing the same as $10$ plums. The second equation represents $1$ apple and $6$ plums weight the same as one pear.

$$\\$$

We substitute for $p$ from the second equation into the first. This gives

$$a + 6m + 3a = 10m$$

Let $a = m$, then we see that $p = 7m$.
