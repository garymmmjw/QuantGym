### 题干

Erica intends to construct a subset $T$ of $S=\{ I,J,K,L,M,N \},$ but if she is unsure about including an element $x$ of $S$ in $T,$ she will write $x$ in bold and include it in $T.$ For example, $\{ I,J \},$ $\{ J,\mathbf{K},L \},$ and $\{ \mathbf{I},\mathbf{J},\mathbf{M},\mathbf{N} \}$ are valid examples of $T,$ while $\{ I,J,\mathbf{J},K \}$ is not. Find the total number of such sets $T$ that Erica can construct. 

### Hint

Each element of $S$ presents Erica with $3$ choices: include, exclude, or bold include.

### 解答

Each element of $S$ presents Erica with $3$ choices: include, exclude, or bold include. As $|S|=6,$ the answer is $3^6=\boxed{729}$
