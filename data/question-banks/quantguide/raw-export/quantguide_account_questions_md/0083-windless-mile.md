### 题干

A man riding on a bike can travel $1$ mile in $3$ minutes if the wind blows in the direction he is moving and in $4$ minutes if it is against him. How fast can the man travel $1$ mile if there is no wind?

### Hint

Thinking about this as a rate, let $x$ be the (in miles/min) that the wind blows and $r$ be the rate (in miles/min) that the man bikes at. Set up the equations in the question in terms of $r$ and $x$.

### 解答

Thinking about this as a rate, let $x$ be the (in miles/min) that the wind blows and $r$ be the rate (in miles/min) that the man bikes at. We know that $r + x = 1/3$ and $r - x = 1/4$ from the question above. Therefore, we get that $r = 7/24$ by solving this system, meaning it would take him $24/7$ minutes to bike a mile windless.
