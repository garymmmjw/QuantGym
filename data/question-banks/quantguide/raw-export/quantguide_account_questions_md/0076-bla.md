### 题干

$$\textbf{Complete Red and Black Urn I Prior!}$ An urn initially consists of $r > 0$ red and $b > 0$ black balls inside. Balls are drawn one-by-one. If the ball drawn is red, return it to the urn. If the ball drawn is black, replace it by a red ball in the urn. The probability that the $n$th ball drawn is red is a function $f(r,b,n)$. Find $f(8,12,11)$ to the nearest hundredth.

### Hint

Use Law of Total Expectation on some indicator

### 解答

The probability of the $n$th ball being red is just $\mathbb{E}[I_n]$, where $I_n$ is the indicator of the event that the $n$th ball is red. We can use Law of Total Expectation to get that $\mathbb{E}[I_n] = \mathbb{E}[\mathbb{E}[I_n \mid X_{n-1}]]$, where $X_{n-1}$ is the number of red balls in the urn after $n-1$ draws. The expectation on the inside is just $\dfrac{X_{n-1}}{r+b}$, as there are $X_{n-1}$ red balls out of $r+b$ total. Therefore, the probability is $\mathbb{E}\left[\dfrac{X_{n-1}}{r+b}\right] = \dfrac{M_{n-1}}{r+b}$. With these particular values, the probability is $\dfrac{M_{10}}{20} \approx 0.64$.
