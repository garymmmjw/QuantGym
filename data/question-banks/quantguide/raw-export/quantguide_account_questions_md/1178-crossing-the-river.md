### 题干

During the Turkish stampede in Thrace, a small detachment found itself confronted by a wide and deep river. However, they discovered a boat in which two children were rowing about. It was so small that it would only carry the two children, or one grown up person. There are a total of $358$ officers and soldiers. How many times does the boat pass from shore to shore if all the officers and soldiers need to get across, and that the two children are the last ones in joint possession of the boat? 

### Hint

How can you get one soldier across and leave both children with the boat? 

### 解答

In order to get one children across the river, proceed as follows:

$$\begin{align*}
&\text{Two children both cross the river in the boat} \\ 
&\text{One child returns with the boat} \\
&\text{One soldier crosses in the boat} \\
&\text{The child on the opposite shore returns with the boat} \\   
\end{align*}$$

We are in the same situation as before, but with one soldier on the opposite bank. There is a total of $358$ people to get across the river. It will therefore take $358 * 4 = 1432$ crossings to get all the men over, leaving the children together with the boat.
