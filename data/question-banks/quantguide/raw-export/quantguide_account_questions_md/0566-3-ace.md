### 题干

You are dealt $5$ cards from a standard deck. You are told at least $3$ of the cards are of the same rank. Find the probability your hand contains all $4$ cards of some rank.

### Hint

There are $48 \cdot 13 = 624$ hands that consist of all $4$ suits of some rank. Using similar logic, how many have at least $3$ of some rank?

### 解答

There are $48 \cdot 13 = 624$ hands that consist of all $4$ suits of some rank. This is because there are $13$ ways to pick the rank in which we have all $4$ suit. Then, there are $48$ ways to pick the last card. We just need to divide this by the total number of hands with at least $3$ cards of some rank. We know $624$ hands have exactly $4$ of some rank. Therefore, we can just count the number of hands with exactly $3$ cards of some rank. There are $13$ ways to pick the rank that we have $3$ of. There are $4$ ways to pick the suit that is excluded from our selection. In addition, there are $\displaystyle \binom{48}{2}$ ways to pick two remaining cards for our hand that are not of the desired rank. Therefore, our answer is $\dfrac{624}{624 + 4 \cdot 13\binom{48}{2}} = \dfrac{1}{95}$.
