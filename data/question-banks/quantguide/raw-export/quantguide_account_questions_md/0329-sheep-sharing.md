### 题干

An Australian farmer dies and leaves his sheep to his three sons. Alfred is to get $20$ percent more than John, and $25$ percent more than Charles. John's share is $3600$ sheep. How many sheep does Charles get? 

### Hint

Create a system of equations 

### 解答

Let $A, J, C$ be the number of sheep each of Alfred, John, and Charles got respectively. We then have the following system:

$$\begin{align*}
&A = J + \frac{20J}{100} \\ 
&A = C + \frac{25J}{100} \\ 
&J = 3600
\end{align*}$$

Solving the system, we get $C = 3456$
