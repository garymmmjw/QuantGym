### 题干

What integer can be multiplied by $1,2,3,4,5,$ and $6$ and no new digits appear in any of the results? For example, $100 \cdot 10 = 1000$ has no new digits appearing in the result after multiplication.

### Hint

What are the properties of the number $7$?

### 解答

This is the recurring part of the decimal expansion of the reciprocal of $7$. 

$$\frac{1}{7} \approx .142857$$

We can see that multiplying these numbers by anything less than $7$ will be some multiple of this, and hence shifts the digits around.
