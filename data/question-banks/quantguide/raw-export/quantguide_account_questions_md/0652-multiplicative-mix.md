### 题干

We have $4$ positive integers $a,b,c,$ and $d$ that satisfy $ab = 21, bc = 15,$ and $cd = 45$. It is also known all of the integers are strictly larger than $1$. Find $a^2 + b^2 + c^2 + d^2$.

### Hint

Write out the prime factorization of $15$ to start.

### 解答

We know that $bc = 15 = 3 \cdot 5$. Since $21$ is not divisible by $5$, this means $b = 3$ and $c = 5$. Since $c = 5$, this means $d = 9$. Since $b = 3$, this means $a = 7$. Adding the squares up, we get that our answer is $3^2 + 5^2 + 7^2 + 9^2 = 164$.
