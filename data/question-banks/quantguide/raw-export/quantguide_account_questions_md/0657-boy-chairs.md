### 题干

$$3$ boys and $3$ girls are in a group. $3$ of these children are randomly selected to be seated in $3$ chairs. Given that all $3$ children selected are all of the same gender, what is the expected amount of boys?

### Hint

What can you say about the probability that all three are boys? What are the possible outcomes?

### 解答

Since there are $3$ boys and $3$ girls and all $3$ children are of the same gender, it is equally likely for all $3$ of the chairs to be occupied by boys and by girls. Therefore, the expected value is just $\dfrac{1}{2} \cdot 3 +\dfrac{1}{2} \cdot 0 = \dfrac{3}{2}$, as it is equally likely for either $3$ or $0$ boys to occupy the chairs.
