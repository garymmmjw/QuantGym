### 题干

Jar $A$ has $3$ blue and $4$ red balls. Jar $B$ has $6$ blue and $4$ red balls. If one ball is selected uniformly at random from each jar, what is the probability that the two selected balls match in color?

### Hint

We can get matching colors by either both being red or both being blue.

### 解答

We can get matching colors by either both being red or both being blue. The probability of two reds is $\dfrac{4}{7} \cdot \dfrac{4}{10} = \dfrac{16}{70}$. The probability of two blues is $\dfrac{3}{7} \cdot \dfrac{6}{10} = \dfrac{18}{70}$. Adding these up, we get our total probability is $\dfrac{16 + 18}{70} = \dfrac{17}{35}$.
