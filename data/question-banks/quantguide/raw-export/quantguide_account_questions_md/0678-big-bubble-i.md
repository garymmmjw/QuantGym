### 题干

A circle has a radius that is increasing at a constant positive rate from $0$. What is the radius at the moment when the circumference and area of the circle are increasing at the same rate?

### Hint

We have that $A(r) = \pi r^2$ and $C(r) = 2\pi r$ represent the area and circumference of a circle as a function of the radius. Take derivatives and set them equal.

### 解答

We have that $A(r) = \pi r^2$ and $C(r) = 2\pi r$ represent the area and circumference of a circle as a function of the radius. We know that $r'$, the rate of increase of the radius, is constant. Therefore, $A' = 2\pi r r'$ and $C' = 2\pi r'$. Setting these equal to each other, $$2\pi r r' = 2 \pi r' \iff r = 1$$
