### 题干

You have $3$ at-the-money options on some different underlyings with strikes $K = 10$, $K = 20$, $K = 30$, all with expiry $T$. Assuming Black-Scholes dynamics, which option (strike) has the largest gamma? 

### Hint

What is the Black-Scholes formula for gamma?

### 解答

Gamma is the largest with the lowest strike. We can think about this in terms of value of the underlying. A $\$1$ move on a $\$10$ stock is a lot more valuable than a $\$1$ move on a $\$1000$ stock.
