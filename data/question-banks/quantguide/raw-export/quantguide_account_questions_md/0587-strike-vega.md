### 题干

You have $3$ at-the-money options on some different underlyings with strikes $K = 10$, $K = 20$, $K = 30$, all with expiry $T$. Assuming Black-Scholes dynamics, which option (strike) has the largest vega? 

### Hint

Are options with higher or lower strikes more valuable? Assuming you can exercise them.

### 解答

Vega is proportional to the strike price when assuming Black-Scholes dynamics. If you can exercise at a larger strike, then the option is more valuable and all things else, should be more sensitive to volatility changes.
