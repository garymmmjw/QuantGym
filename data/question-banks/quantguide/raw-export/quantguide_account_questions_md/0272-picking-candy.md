### 题干

Your older brother places 5 good candies and 5 bad candies in front of you, as well as two boxes. He asks you to put the candies into these two boxes, and he will, blindfolded, choose a random candy from a random box, which you get to keep. You strategize to put the candies into the boxes in such a way that maximizes your chances of receiving a good candy. With this strategy, what is the probability that you receive a good candy?

### Hint

Your optimal strategy is to put one good candy in one box, and the remainder of the candy in the other box.

### 解答

Your optimal strategy is to put one good candy in one box, and the remainder of the candy in the other box. The probability that you receive a good candy is $\frac{1}{2} \times 1 + \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{4}{9} = \frac{13}{18}.$
