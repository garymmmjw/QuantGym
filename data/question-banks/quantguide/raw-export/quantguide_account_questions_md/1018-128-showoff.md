### 题干

Suppose you have a fair $8-$sided die and roll it once. You can either keep the value showing on that die or roll a fair $12-$sided die once. You receive the payout of the last die you roll. Find your expected payout under optimal play.

### Hint

What is the expected value of the $12-$sided die? Using this, when will we want to re-roll?

### 解答

The expected value of the $12-$sided die is $\dfrac{1+12}{2} = \dfrac{13}{2}$. Therefore, we would want to keep our original roll if it is a $7$ or $8$. Otherwise, we will roll the $12-$sided die. Therefore, our expected value is $$\dfrac{7+8}{8} + \dfrac{3}{4} \cdot \dfrac{13}{2} = \dfrac{27}{4}$$ as we will roll the $12-$sided die with probability $\dfrac{3}{4}$.
