### 题干

A snail is $30$ feet away from some food. The snail moves at a rate of $4$ feet per hour. However, at the end of each hour, a large gust of wind blows and the snail moves back $2$ feet. The snail eats the food instantaneously once it reaches it. How many hours does it take for the snail to reach the food?

### Hint

For the first $13$ hours, the snail moves up $4$ feet and then gets blown back $2$ feet, moving at a net rate of $2$ feet per hour. 

### 解答

For the first $13$ hours, the snail moves up $4$ feet and then gets blown back $2$ feet, moving at a net rate of $2$ feet per hour. Thus, after $13$ hours, the snail is $26$ feet away from the starting point. Then, in the $14$th hour, the snail will reach the $30$ foot mark and eat the food instantaneously, so it takes $14$ hours for the snail to eat the food.
