### 题干

There are 100 light bulbs in a room, each initially off and with its switch by its side. The first person enters and flips every switch. The second person enters and flips every other switch. The third person enters and flips every third switch. This process continues until the 100-th person flips every 100-th switch, or the last switch. How many lights are on?

### Hint

Come up with some examples where the light remains on. What causes the light to remain on at the end of the process?

### 解答

The $i$-th bulb is switched by person $j$ if $j$ is a factor of $i$. Thus, the $i$-th bulb will remain on if it has an odd number of factors. We call a number that has an odd number of factors a perfect square, and there are 10 perfect squares from 1 to 100.
